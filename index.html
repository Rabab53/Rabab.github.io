Biography

I am a postdoc in the Julia Lab located in the Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). My research is centered around task-based numerical libraries and applications, performance optimizations for multicore/manycore architectures and hardware accelerators, dynamic runtime systems, GPU programming, and machine learning and artificial intelligence. I was recently selected as one of Rising Stars in Computational and Data Sciences by Sandia National laboratories. During my PhD study, I worked with many collaborators from International laboratories such as Innovative Computing Lab in University of Tennessee and Material Forming in MINES ParisTech University. In Summer 2021, I joined Innovative Computing Lab at the University of Tennessee as visiting scholar. There I worked towards one of Software for Linear Algebra Targeting Exascale (SLATE) project milestones which is a joint project of the U.S. Department of Energyâ€™s Office of Science and National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA). I received prestigious Gauss Award for Supercomputing of Excellence from ISC High Performance Computing Conference, and KAUST Research Excellence Award for academic year 21/20. In KAUST-NVIDIA workshop, I achieved 1st place in the Artificial Intelligence competition based on the geospatial dataset. I received two poster awards one in Artificial Intelligence in Medicine conference at KAUST and another one in Saudi HPC conference at King Abdulaziz University.
